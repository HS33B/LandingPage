SOME RANDOM INFORMATION

Cheetahs are the fastest land animals, capable of reaching speeds up to 70 miles per hour in short bursts covering distances up to 1,500 feet. They have slender bodies and long legs built for speed.
Bald eagles are powerful birds of prey found in North America. They have a wingspan of up to 7.5 feet and are known for their distinctive white head and tail feathers.
Penguins are highly adapted to aquatic life, with wings that have evolved into flippers for swimming. They cannot fly but are exceptional swimmers, often reaching speeds of up to 15 miles per hour in the water.
African elephants are the largest land animals on Earth, with males reaching heights of up to 13 feet and weighing as much as 12,000 pounds. They are known for their large ears which help them regulate their body temperature.
